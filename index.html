Tax Treaties in Mozambique

Mozambique is losing a lot of money because of their current tax treaties. In this application we are going to tell you more about this problem.

How much money is Mozambique losing?

The total lost revenue due to no tax being paid on dividends and interest between 2010-2020 is an estimated US$1.4 billion. Per year, Mozambique will miss about US$128 million in WHT revenue, which is about 0.90% of gross domestic product.

What are tax treaties?

A tax treaty is an agreement made between two countries for the purpose of making trading easier and preventing international double taxation.

Treaties made between developing countries and rich countries are often unfavorable to developing countries. These unfavorable treaties cause money to flow from developing countries to rich countries virtually tax-free.

Why is this a problem?

Because Mozambique has entered into unfavorable tax treaties with a number of countries, it is losing a lot of money.

Large international companies trade with Mozambique from these countries so they don't have to pay much, if any, tax. This is a shame and unnecessary because large amounts of money are invested in Mozambique's gas reserves. Because of the current tax treaties, Mozambique barely makes any money from this.

What if the tax treaties were better?

If Mozambique had better tax treaties there would now be much more money available by the government. The government could then use the money for such things as: education, healthcare, military, housing and infrastructure.

How to solve this problem?

Stop current negotiations and first establish a Double Tax Agreement (DTA) policy favorable to Mozambique.

Stop making unfair tax treaties with rich countries.

Review existing tax treaties and stop/ renegotiate if they are not favorable.

When entering into new treaties, ensure that taxes are not too low as in previous treaties.
